# PGA Tour Game Scenario Copy

Use this as an editable copy bank for regular season, major, playoff, and FedEx Cup outcomes.

Placeholder notes:
- `<player>`: player name
- `<tournament>`: tournament name
- `<major>`: major name
- `<category>`: strokes gained category
- `<finish>`: finishing position
- `<rank>`: FedEx Cup rank or seed
- `<amount>`: money/prize value

## Regular Season

### Top 5 Finish In Regular Season

Trigger: Player finishes regular season ranked 1-5.

Copy:
A monster regular season lands you among the very top seeds heading into the FedEx Cup Playoffs. Expectations are high.

Alternate:
You did not sneak into the postseason. You kicked the door in. A top-five regular season means the whole Tour knows you are dangerous.

Alternate:
That is the kind of regular season that changes the way people talk about you. Now comes the hard part: making it hold up in August.

### Top 10 Finish In Regular Season

Trigger: Player finishes regular season ranked 6-10.

Copy:
You spent the regular season living near the top of leaderboards. You have done enough to matter. Now you need three good weeks to turn a great year into a famous one.

Alternate:
Top 10 in the standings is not an accident. You built a season with real weight behind it, and the playoffs are ready to test it.

### Top 30 Finish In Regular Season

Trigger: Player finishes regular season ranked 11-30.

Copy:
You are safely inside the group everyone is chasing. No panic, no miracle needed. Just keep the good golf from turning into nervous golf.

Alternate:
You enter the playoffs in real position, not just with hope. A few sharp rounds could make this season look much bigger than it did in June.

### Bubble Into Top 70

Trigger: Player finishes regular season ranked 61-70.

Copy:
You made it to the postseason with almost no room to spare. It does not have to be pretty, but it does have to be better from here.

Alternate:
That was a tense way to get in, but you are in. The scorecard does not ask how comfortable you were.

### Miss Top 70

Trigger: Player finishes regular season outside top 70.

Copy:
The regular season never quite gave you enough. There were flashes, but the playoffs needed more than flashes.

Alternate:
You had your moments, but not enough of them stacked together. The FedEx Cup moves on without you this time.

### Win But Outside Top 30 Or SG Total Under 0.3

Trigger: Player won a regular season event but finished outside top 30 or had SG Total under 0.3.

Copy:
Four great days can compensate for an otherwise okay season. The win takes care of the stress, but if you get hot at the right time you can make it a great season. Let's see what you've got.

Alternate:
The season was uneven, but a trophy changes the whole conversation. You already proved you can beat everyone for one week. The question is whether you can do it again when the lights get brighter.

### Multiple Wins

Trigger: Player won two or more regular season events.

Copy:
Winning once can be a heater. Winning more than once is a statement. For Tiger it was a great month, for most it's a great career. For you it's a great season.

Alternate:
Two trophies before the postseason means nobody gets to call this a fluke. You have been the answer more than once already.

### No Wins But Runner-Up

Trigger: Player had no wins and at least one second-place finish.

Copy:
You almost got in the win column this year. A runnder up at this level is great, and you have a chance coming into the most important part of the year. Good luck.

Alternate:
No trophy, but you got close enough to feel the heat. Let's hope you don't melt in the Playoffs.

### No Wins, Top 3 Finish, Outside Top 70

Trigger: Player had no wins, at least one top-three finish, and finished outside top 70.

Copy:
A couple of lip-ins instead of lip-outs and you would not have to sweat next year at all, but the breaks did not go your way at the `<tournament>`.

Alternate:
The `<tournament>` showed what was possible, then the rest of the year showed how thin the margin can be. Close was not quite enough.

### Consistent Top 10s, No Win

Trigger: Player had three or more top-10 finishes and no wins.

Copy:
You kept finding the first page of the leaderboard, but never quite the last handshake. The floor is real. Now you need the ceiling to show up.

Alternate:
There is a lot to like in a season full of top 10s. There is also one empty spot on the resume that still bothers you.

### Feast Or Famine Season

Trigger: Player had a win or top three, but also several missed cuts or low finishes.

Copy:
When it was good, it was very good. When it was bad, it got expensive fast. The playoffs will find out which version packed the clubs.

Alternate:
This season had a highlight reel and a few pages you would rather skip. Volatility can win, but it can also send you home early.

### Strong Finish To Regular Season

Trigger: Player gained ground late in the regular season or finished with recent top results.

Copy:
You found something late, which is exactly when everyone else starts checking the standings too much. Momentum is not a trophy, but it is useful.

Alternate:
The best golf of your season showed up near the deadline. That is either perfect timing or a very convincing setup.

### Late Fade

Trigger: Player started strong but dropped late or missed late opportunities.

Copy:
The early season bought you room. The late season spent most of it. You are still alive, but the trend line is not doing you favors.

Alternate:
You had enough good golf banked to survive the fade. The playoffs will not be as forgiving.

### Breakout Season

Trigger: Player significantly outperformed prior baseline or had first notable season.

Copy:
This was the season people stopped scrolling past your name. The game has changed. Now you get to see how it feels when expectations arrive.

Alternate:
You turned potential into standings points. That is a big step, and the next one is usually harder.

### Quietly Solid Season

Trigger: Player made the playoffs without many high finishes.

Copy:
No fireworks, not much drama, just enough good golf to keep moving. There are worse ways to arrive in the playoffs.

Alternate:
This was not a season built for posters, but it was built well enough to last. You are still here.

### Statistical Monster, No Win

Trigger: Player had high SG Total but no win.

Copy:
The numbers say you played like someone who should have won. Golf, being golf, did not bother to agree. The playoffs owe you nothing, but you are due a run.

Alternate:
Your strokes gained profile looks better than your trophy case. That can be frustrating. It can also be a warning sign for everyone else.

### Bad Stats, Good Results

Trigger: Player had weak SG Total but strong points or finishes.

Copy:
The stat sheet is not exactly glowing, but the standings still count. You got enough out of the season. Now you need the golf to catch up with the results.

Alternate:
It was not always convincing, but it worked. The playoffs are less patient with magic tricks.

At the `<major>`, where it mattered most, you showed up. Your Wikipedia major grid is getting a bit of color.

Alternate:
A top 10 at the `<major>` travels well. That is the kind of week that makes a season feel sturdier.

Copy:
You are going down in the history books. You won one of the ones that really count.

Alternate:
There are wins, and then there are wins with their own Wikipedia pages. The `<major>` changed your career.

### Runner-Up In A Major

Trigger: Player finished second in a major.

Copy:
You were one step from forever at the `<major>`. That one is going to replay in your head, but it also proves the stage is not too big.

Alternate:
Second in a major hurts because it should. It also means you were close enough to make everyone nervous.

### Missed Cut In A Major

Trigger: Player missed cut in a major.

Copy:
The `<major>` did not wait around for you to find it. Big weeks punish small mistakes quickly.

Alternate:

### Strong Major Season

Trigger: Player had multiple major top 20s or one top 10 plus another solid result.

Copy:
The majors kept finding your name in respectable places. That is not a trophy, but it is not nothing.

Alternate:
Across the biggest weeks, you looked like you belonged. That matters when the postseason starts asking bigger questions.

## Playoffs

### Advanced To BMW Championship

Trigger: Player survives first playoff event and reaches top 50.

Copy:
You made it through the first checkpoint. The field is smaller, the points are louder, and every loose swing gets more expensive.

Alternate:
Round one is handled. Now the season gets sharper, and so does the math.

### Advanced To Tour Championship

Trigger: Player reaches top 30.

Copy:
You are going to East Lake. That means the season is officially more than solid. Now it is a chance at the whole thing.

Alternate:
Top 30 gets you into the room where the FedEx Cup is decided. You earned the invite. Now earn the noise.

### Eliminated In First Playoff Event

Trigger: Player misses top 50 after the first playoff event.

Copy:
The postseason did not last long enough. You had a seat at the table, but the first event took it back.

Alternate:
One playoff week was all you got. That is a hard stop after a long season.

### Eliminated Before Tour Championship

Trigger: Player reaches BMW but misses top 30.

Copy:
You got through one gate but not the last one. East Lake was close enough to picture and too far away to touch.

Alternate:
The season ends one step before the finale. Good year, tough exit.

### Won First Playoff Event

Trigger: Player wins the first FedEx Cup Playoffs event.

Copy:
That is how you start a postseason. One week in, and you have made the bracket feel like it belongs to you.

Alternate:
Opening the playoffs with a win changes everything. The points jump, the confidence jumps, and so does everyone else's blood pressure.

### Won BMW Championship

Trigger: Player wins second playoff event.

Copy:
You won the event that sends people to East Lake with real belief. That is not momentum. That is a warning.

Alternate:
The BMW Championship is a very good time to look like the best player in the field.

### Won Tour Championship

Trigger: Player wins final playoff event.

Copy:
You saved the best week for the last week. That is as clean as a season ending gets.

Alternate:
The finale asked for your best and you brought it. Nobody gets to rewrite that ending.

### Won FedEx Cup

Trigger: Player wins the FedEx Cup.

Copy:
That is the whole thing. The regular season, the playoffs, the math, the pressure, the final leaderboard. You won the FedEx Cup.

Alternate:
The season ends with your name on top and everyone else doing hypotheticals. That is a pretty good way to finish.

### Lost FedEx Cup After Entering As Favorite

Trigger: Player enters Tour Championship as top seed but does not win.

Copy:
You arrived with the best seat in the house and still watched someone else lift the trophy. That one will sting.

Alternate:
Starting position gave you the advantage. The final week took it away.

### Low Seed Makes Deep Run

Trigger: Player enters playoffs outside top 50 but reaches Tour Championship or finishes high.

Copy:
You were not supposed to be a major part of the playoff story. Then you kept advancing anyway.

Alternate:
This started as a survival mission and turned into something much more interesting.

### Final Round Charge

Trigger: Player makes a big move in the final round of a playoff event.

Copy:
Sunday finally gave you the round you needed. Nothing like making the math nervous at the last possible second.

Alternate:
You made your move late enough that everyone else had to notice.

### Final Round Fade

Trigger: Player loses position or gets eliminated after poor final round.

Copy:
The week was right there until Sunday started taking pieces away. That is a brutal way to leave.

Alternate:
You gave yourself a chance, then watched it leak out over the closing holes.

The driver showed up with playoff confidence. Fairways felt wider than they had any right to feel.

Copy:

Alternate:

Alternate:

No single part of the bag carried you, and no single part wrecked you. Sometimes the playoffs are just about whether the whole thing holds together.

Alternate:
This was a balanced playoff run. No fireworks from one category, no disaster from another. Just the full bag trying to survive.

The whole playoff run had a lead actor, and it was `<category>`. When the rest of the game got shaky, that part kept answering.

Alternate:
You rode `<category>` as far as it would take you. For a while, that was pretty far.

### One Category Sinks The Run

Trigger: One category is clearly worst relative to typical value.

Copy:
The difference between advancing and packing up was hiding in `<category>`. That part of the game picked a bad week to disappear.

## Composite Endings

### Great Season, Great Playoffs

Trigger: Strong regular season and deep playoff finish.

Copy:
This was the full-season version. You built the position, protected it, and still had enough left when the field got smaller.

Alternate:
Some seasons look good in pieces. This one looks good from far away too.

### Great Season, Bad Playoffs

Trigger: Strong regular season but early playoff exit.

Copy:
The regular season was too good to ignore, but the ending came too fast. That is the problem with playoff golf: it edits the story aggressively.

Alternate:
You did the long part right and the short part wrong. Unfortunately, the short part gets the final word.

### Average Season, Great Playoffs

Trigger: Middling regular season but deep playoff run.

Copy:
The regular season did not shout your name. The playoffs did. Timing can make a season look completely different.

Alternate:
You were fine for months, then dangerous when it mattered. Nobody complains about peaking late.

### Average Season, Average Playoffs

Trigger: Middling regular season and ordinary playoff result.

Copy:
This was a professional season. Not a disaster, not a breakthrough, just enough good golf to keep the lights on.

Alternate:
You were in the mix, but never really in control of it. Some years are like that.

### Poor Season, Surprise Playoff Moment

Trigger: Weak regular season but one notable playoff result or round.

Copy:
Most of the year was a grind, but you found one playoff moment worth saving. Sometimes that is enough to keep the offseason from feeling too long.

Alternate:
It was not a great season, but it was not empty either. That late spark gives you something to carry forward.

### Missed Playoffs But Won Event

Trigger: Player won an event but still missed playoffs.

Copy:
A win should make a season feel secure. This one did not. The trophy is real, but so is the missed postseason.

Alternate:
You got the best Sunday feeling in golf and still ran out of season. That is a strange little line on the resume.

### No Wins, Deep Playoff Run

Trigger: Player did not win in regular season but reached Tour Championship or contended late.

Copy:
You never got the regular season trophy, but you kept giving yourself chances until the season became serious.

Alternate:
Winning is the cleanest proof. A deep playoff run is not bad evidence either.

### First Win Changes The Season

Trigger: Player earns first Tour win.

Copy:
That first win changes the way the whole season feels. The question is not whether you can do it anymore. It is how often.

Alternate:
First wins are heavy. You carried this one all the way into the postseason conversation.

### Career Year

Trigger: Player sets personal best in rank, wins, money, or SG Total.

Copy:
This was the kind of season that resets expectations. Career years are fun until they become the new standard.

Alternate:
You just gave future-you a harder job. That is usually a good sign.

### Big Money Finish

Trigger: Player earns major prize or large playoff payout.

Copy:
The leaderboard was nice. The direct deposit was nicer. You made the season pay off.

Alternate:
That is a lot of green for a few very stressful rounds of golf.

### Narrow Miss

Trigger: Player misses a target rank or advancement by a small margin.

Copy:
One shot here, one better bounce there, and this season reads completely differently. Golf loves making math feel personal.

Alternate:
You were close enough that it is almost worse. Almost does not advance, but it does linger.

### Dominant Statistical Season

Trigger: Player leads or nearly leads SG Total.

Copy:
The numbers were not subtle. Tee to green, green to cup, week to week, you played like one of the best players alive.

Alternate:
There are seasons where the stats need explaining. This was not one of them.

### Scrappy Survival Season

Trigger: Player makes postseason or advances despite weak overall profile.

Copy:
This was not elegant, but it survived. Sometimes the season is less about style and more about refusing to leave.

Alternate:
You kept finding just enough. That can be annoying for everyone else, which is part of the charm.
